Use #1: Habitual Actions

We use the present simple to talk about habits or routines — things that we often do on a regular basis. We often use adverbs of frequency (e.g. always, often, usually, sometimes, seldom, never, etc.) with the present simple.

Examples
  • I usually go to the gym in the morning.
  • She always forgets her keys.
  • They seldom eat fast food.
  • We never miss a chance to travel.

Use #2: Facts or Generalizations

The present simple is also used to express facts or generalizations — things that are always true, or things that happen over and over.

Examples
  • Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
  • The Earth revolves around the Sun.
  • Children love toys.
  • People need air to live.
